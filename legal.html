// Section 01

Privacy notice

SentryOne recognises that operational systems can contain sensitive information, including personal information about staff, guards, clients, contractors, visitors, and incident participants. SentryOne will handle personal information in accordance with applicable New Zealand law and its contractual obligations.

Privacy-focused handlingCommercial SaaS contextOperational recordsReasonable security controls
// Section 02

Information SentryOne may collect

Supplied directly

  • Account, billing, and contact details
  • Tenant, company, and site information
  • Uploaded documents, patrol logs, incidents, notes, photos, and attachments
  • Support requests, training requests, and correspondence

Generated through use

  • Audit logs, access records, activity timestamps, and role changes
  • Device, browser, app, and session metadata
  • Performance, security, and troubleshooting telemetry
  • Report generation, notification, and workflow events

SentryOne does not need users to submit more personal information than is reasonably required for service delivery, security, billing, support, lawful compliance, or agreed product functions.

// Section 03

How information may be used

  • To create and manage accounts, tenants, subscriptions, and permissions
  • To host, operate, secure, and improve the service
  • To generate reports, notifications, logs, and user-requested outputs
  • To detect fraud, misuse, unauthorised access, or platform abuse
  • To provide onboarding, support, maintenance, and service communications
  • To comply with legal obligations, lawful requests, and dispute processes

SentryOne may also use de-identified and aggregated service information for analytics, capacity planning, product development, resilience testing, and quality improvement, provided those outputs do not reasonably identify a person or customer.

// Section 04

Disclosure, hosting partners, and subprocessors

SentryOne may disclose or make information available to carefully selected third-party service providers where reasonably necessary to deliver the service. This can include hosting providers, email and notification services, analytics or monitoring providers, authentication services, storage providers, and technical support partners.

  • Third parties are only given access to the extent reasonably necessary
  • Access is subject to contractual or operational safeguards where appropriate
  • Information may also be disclosed where required by law, court order, regulator direction, or urgent safety need

If SentryOne becomes aware of unlawful misuse, credible threats, fraud, or incidents affecting system integrity, it may preserve and disclose relevant records to advisers, insurers, investigators, law enforcement, or regulators where reasonably necessary.

// Section 05

Security, retention, and breach response

SentryOne applies reasonable security controls appropriate to a commercial hosted platform. Those controls may include authentication controls, privilege management, monitoring, encrypted transport, environment separation, backups, logging, patching, and restricted administrative access.

Retention

Information is retained for as long as reasonably necessary for service delivery, legal compliance, dispute handling, fraud prevention, backup integrity, or agreed transition periods. Data may then be deleted, anonymised, or irreversibly de-identified.

Privacy incidents

If SentryOne identifies a material privacy or security incident affecting customer information, it will take reasonable steps to contain, assess, and respond to the incident, including notifying affected customers where appropriate and legally required.

// Section 06

Access, correction, and account rights

Where personal information is held by SentryOne in its own right, individuals may request access to or correction of that information through the relevant SentryOne contact channel.

Where SentryOne holds personal information only on behalf of a customer, SentryOne may direct the request to that customer or require the request to be made through the customer's own privacy contact, because the customer controls the operational record.

  • Identity verification may be required before releasing information
  • Requests may be limited where lawful grounds for withholding apply
  • Correction requests may be recorded alongside the original record where appropriate
// Section 07

Website use, cookies, and communications

SentryOne's website may use cookies, local storage, analytics tags, or similar technologies for login continuity, preference storage, security, abuse prevention, traffic analysis, and performance monitoring.

  • You can usually manage cookies through your browser settings
  • Blocking some technologies may affect login, forms, or core website functions
  • Operational service notices may still be sent where needed for account, billing, security, or support purposes

Marketing or promotional communications should include an unsubscribe or opt-out method where required.

// Section 08

Compliance, misuse, and no legal guarantee

SentryOne is designed to support security and operational workflows, but no software page, workflow, report, or automation can by itself guarantee legal compliance, evidential sufficiency, incident outcome, commercial success, or risk elimination.

Customers remain responsible for their own employment obligations, surveillance policies, records handling, contractual duties, site-specific rules, health and safety practices, and any decisions made based on information recorded in the platform.

SentryOne may revise this page from time to time to reflect platform updates, legal developments, risk management decisions, or operational changes.

// Section 09

Legal notices and complaints

Formal legal notices, takedown requests, infringement complaints, privacy requests, and authorised law-enforcement communications should be sent through SentryOne's designated official contact path or as otherwise stated in the applicable signed agreement.

When making a legal complaint or notice, include enough detail to identify the affected account, tenant, record, or content, together with the legal basis of the request and your authority to make it.
