What this help page covers

SentryOne support is intended to help customers use the platform as supplied, restore normal service where reasonably possible, answer product questions, investigate defects, and provide practical guidance for supported workflows.

  • Login and access issues
  • User setup and permissions questions
  • Incident, patrol, report, and portal workflow issues
  • Notifications, exports, display problems, and suspected bugs
  • Service availability or degraded performance concerns

Support is provided on a reasonable efforts basis unless a separate signed support schedule or service level agreement states otherwise.

// Section 02

How to log an issue properly

To help SentryOne investigate quickly, include as much of the following as possible:

  • Your company or tenant name
  • The affected user name and role
  • The page or feature affected
  • The time the problem occurred
  • What you expected to happen
  • What actually happened
  • Any error message shown
  • Device type, browser, operating system, or app version
  • Screenshots, screen recordings, or record IDs where available

For urgent issues, include the business impact clearly — for example, whether users are blocked from logging incidents, accessing client data, or completing patrol workflows.

// Section 05

Customer responsibilities during support

  • Nominate appropriate points of contact
  • Provide accurate reproduction steps and relevant evidence
  • Maintain local internet, browser, device, and endpoint security hygiene
  • Apply recommended account, permission, and password controls
  • Cooperate with reasonable diagnostic steps and testing requests

SentryOne may be unable to resolve an issue promptly where required information is not supplied, third-party dependencies are failing, or the issue is caused by unsupported local customisations, unsupported devices, or customer-side infrastructure problems.

// Section 06

Work not included in standard support

The following are not included in standard support unless separately agreed in writing:

  • Custom software development or major workflow redesign
  • Data cleansing, bulk migration, or retrospective data repair
  • Formal legal advice, evidential review, or records compliance advice
  • Support for third-party hardware, third-party software, ISP issues, or unmanaged customer networks
  • Project management, consulting, training programmes, or on-site services beyond what has been sold
// Section 07

Quick self-help checklist before logging a ticket

  • Refresh the page or sign out and back in
  • Check whether the user has the correct role and permission set
  • Test in an up-to-date supported browser
  • Confirm internet connectivity and whether the issue affects one user or many
  • Capture the exact error and the record or page involved
  • Check whether a report filter, date range, or status filter is hiding the expected data

Where a problem may involve privacy, security, or unauthorised access, treat it as urgent and escalate immediately through your designated support path.
